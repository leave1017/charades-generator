Charades for Kids – 60 Easy Words for Classroom & Playdates

Charades is one of the best screen-free activities you can pull out for kids — no materials, no setup, just silliness and laughter. The key is picking words that are genuinely actable for the age group. Here are 60 tried-and-tested charades words for kids, sorted by age so every child gets a fair shot.

How to Run Charades in a Classroom

Charades in a classroom setting is different from charades at a party — you need the whole class engaged, not just the team whose turn it is. Here's a structure that works:

1️⃣
Split into two teams
Teams of 4–6 work best. Too small and you run out of guessers, too large and kids disengage. Mixed ages work well — younger kids can guess even if they can't act yet.
2️⃣
Use a visible timer
Project a countdown on the board, or use a loud kitchen timer. Kids love the urgency — it keeps everyone watching even when it's not their turn.
3️⃣
Write words on cards in advance
Prep 20–30 cards before the session. Keep two separate decks — easier cards for younger kids, harder ones for older groups. Draw from the appropriate deck per actor.
4️⃣
Rotate actors every round
Every child should act at least once. Go around the room in order so no one gets skipped. Kids who are shy often surprise everyone once they're up.

Kid-Friendly Rule Adjustments

Standard charades rules can frustrate young players. Here's what to tweak:

  • → Allow sound effects for ages 4–6. A roaring lion or a honking car helps them express concepts they don't have the motor skills to mime precisely.
  • → Skip the category signal (the "movie" hand gesture, etc.) unless kids already know it. Focus on acting and guessing, not the meta-signals.
  • → Give a pass option. If a child freezes, let them pass without losing a point. The goal is fun, not pressure.
  • → Double the timer for younger actors. 60 seconds instead of 30 keeps the game moving without leaving kids stranded.
  • → Let a friend stand with them. For very shy or young kids, a buddy can whisper encouragement and help them think of how to act the word.
